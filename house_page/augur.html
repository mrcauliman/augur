What it does

You give it
  • Public wallet addresses
  • Chain selection (XRPL first)
  • Optional labels for clarity
It gives you
  • Plain-English statements
  • Organized snapshots and summaries
  • Exportable data for your own records

How it works

  1. Add public addresses
  2. AUGUR reads public chain history only
  3. AUGUR generates statements and exports
No signing, no seed phrases, no private keys. If a future feature needs authentication, it happens outside the oracle and stays optional.

Quick facts

  • Read-only means it never moves funds
  • Public data only means no secrets required
  • No wallet connection means no popups, no approvals
  • No keys means nothing to steal
  • No custody means you stay in control
